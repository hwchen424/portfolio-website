Problem Statement

Incoming USF freshmen and visitors find it difficult to navigate around campus. This can be especially stressful since University can be a fast-paced environment where punctuality is a necessary part of everyday campus life. As such, not being able to find the location of your classes, printers or cafes can be a huge impediment to a student’s day.

Interviews

We interviewed Freshman and Sophomore Students that live on campus since these two groups made up the majority of students that lived on campus. The questions that we asked were used to determine the following about the participants:

  • Their knowledge of campus buildings
  • Their perceived ease of getting from point A to B
  • Their perceived ease of getting from point A to B

Personas

We developed two personas to represent the two cohorts that were interviewed. These are the personas.

User Profile 1: Freshman

  • They have low knowledge of campus buildings due to unfamiliarity with school
  • They have moderate ease of getting from one part of campus to another when it came to classes
  • They have a high familiarity with using google maps and other apps

User Profile 2: Sophomore

  • They have mid-high knowledge of campus locations
  • Many of the sophomores live off campus
  • High familiarity with using apps
